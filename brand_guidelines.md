## 3. Brand Identity

The following describes how Ritchie Bros. branding should be practiced when communicating for the brand and marketplace.

### Logo

The Ritchie Bros. logo is comprised of two graphic elements — a **symbol** and a **logotype**.

- **Symbol:** a stylized "RB" monogram; cornerstone of the brand identity.
- **Logotype:** a customized arrangement of typography.

Locked up together, they communicate for the organization as a whole.

#### Logo variants

When the full-color logo isn't feasible, alternatives include:

- Reversed version with an orange RB
- Black and white rendition

Ensure logos exhibit strong contrast and sit on a background that is uncluttered and free from distractions.

#### Clear-space & minimum sizing (full logo)

- Exclusion zone = **width of the "o"**.
- No other elements (text or graphics) within this zone.
- Clear space applies to the entire outer edge.
- Clear space may be increased, never reduced.
- Never render the logo smaller than the minimum size shown in the source PDF.
- **Aspect ratio (CRITICAL):** native 264 × 41 px (≈ **6.44 : 1** width-to-height). When placing the logo on a slide or any layout, **preserve this ratio** — set width OR height and let the renderer derive the other (or compute height = width ÷ 6.44). Never set width and height independently; doing so stretches the lockup vertically, which is one of the misuse modes listed below and is immediately visible on a finished slide.

#### RB Symbol (standalone)

The RB symbol establishes an emotional connection with consumers and encapsulates Ritchie Bros.' history. Applications include building facades, favicons, and apparel. Whenever feasible, pair it with the full Ritchie Bros. logo.

#### Clear-space & minimum sizing (RB symbol)

- Exclusion zone = **height of the "r"**.
- Same rules as the full logo: no elements inside the zone, applied to the outer edge, never reduced.

#### Logo misuse — do NOT

- Change the typeface
- Apply colour (beyond approved variants)
- Stack elements
- Rearrange elements
- Adjust the size of individual elements
- Add sub-brand logos to the lockup
- Typeset the RB symbol
- Use the old Ritchie Bros. logo

### Brand Typography

Primary typeface: **Eina 01** (https://extratype.com/fonts/eina).

Consistent use of a single typeface streamlines and strengthens the brand.

Weights available:

- Eina 01 – Light
- Eina 01 – Regular
- Eina 01 – Semibold
- Eina 01 – Bold

**Fallbacks** (when Eina is unavailable): Roboto, Arial (Microsoft Windows 3.1 and on).

#### Typesetting hierarchy

Headlines use **Eina 01 Bold**; body copy uses **Eina 01 Regular**.

### Design Application (print / marketing)

Marketing materials should maintain a uniform, polished appearance. Headlines must convey a distinct message; images should be striking and engaging within the layout.

### Digital Design Application

Digital designs should be informative, with lucid messaging and captivating photography. The palette should be predominantly minimal, with **subtle accents of orange**.

### Illustrations

Style: simple yet refined.

- Combine line and solid shapes.
- Line weights must be uniform throughout an illustration.
- Use solid shapes for emphasis or form where needed.
- Limit the colour palette to **1–2 colours**.
- Reduce visual complexity as much as possible.
- Consider knocking out line work when overlapped by shapes for visual variety.

### Iconography

Use the **Material Design System Icons & Symbols** sets (https://fonts.google.com/icons) for digital applications.

### UI Components

- Spacious, simple, minimal graphic elements.
- Black is the dominant colour; use tints of black for inactive or hover states.
- Use accent colours (orange) **sparingly**, only when extra visual emphasis is needed.

## 4. Image Guidelines

Imagery covers three subject groups: **people, equipment, and technology**.

Overall feel:

- Dynamic; conveys positive energy.
- High contrast, but the dynamic range between light and dark should not appear surreal.
- Colours vibrant, but not heavily saturated or visually overpowering.
- Prefer Ritchie Bros.' own photos (employee, branch, and office shoots). Stock photography is acceptable when needed.

### People

The heart of Ritchie Bros. is people — employees, partners, and customers worldwide. Use people-focused photography that illuminates integrity, honesty, innovation, and professionalism. Photos may be used in **black and white or full colour**.

### Equipment

Tens of thousands of people count on Ritchie Bros. every year to buy, sell, and manage equipment and assets. Convey this visually with asset-focused photography that is dynamic, varied in scale and industry, and **honest in tone** (not fantastic or overly surreal).

## Quick reference for the creative automation tool

Distilled defaults for use when auto-generating branded ad variations per `PLAN.md`:

- **Logo:** use the locked-up full-colour logo when possible; on busy photography, fall back to the reversed (orange RB) or white monochrome version for contrast.
- **Logo clear-space:** keep a margin equal to the width of the "o" in the logotype around the lockup. Never place text or graphics inside this zone.
- **Logo aspect ratio (CRITICAL):** native 264 × 41 px (≈ 6.44 : 1). Always preserve this ratio when sizing the logo — set width OR height, never both. Stretching it vertically is the most common misuse and is immediately visible. For a typical bottom-right corner placement on a 1920 × 1080 slide, ~180–260 px wide is a safe target (height auto-derives to ~28–40 px).
- **Headline font:** Eina 01 Bold (fallback: Roboto Bold, then Arial Bold).
- **Body font:** Eina 01 Regular (fallback: Roboto, then Arial).
- **Palette ratios:** mostly black/white, with orange `#E87511` limited to ~10–15% as a trim/accent.
- **Text colour on photos:** prefer white with a subtle dark scrim for legibility; accent with orange sparingly.
- **Image tone:** high contrast but not surreal; vibrant but not oversaturated.
